The fan-shaped and calcified green seaweed Udotea geppiorum is typically found in sandy substrates.

The green seaweed Caulerpa lentillifera, locally known as ‘lato’ or ‘ar-arusip’, is an economically important species that is widely consumed as salad in the Philippines. The species is typically harvested from the wild but is also cultivated in several areas in the country such as in Mactan, Cebu.

Codium species, locally known as ‘popoklo’, are known for their spongy texture. Popoklo are considered to be food delicacy and are consumed raw or in salad especially in northern Philippines.

Economically important red seaweed Asparagopsis taxiformis, a species that produce bioactives that can significantly reduce the amount of methane belched by cows. The species is currently being developed for mass production as feed additive.

The red seaweed Halymenia durvillei is common in rocky areas in many tropical reefs including the Philippines. It is known to produce high-value natural products such as carrageenan and pigments r-phycoerythrin and r-phycocyanin. Due to its economic potential a culture technology for the species was recently developed by Dr. Gavino C. Trono Jr. and his team.

A pedazo made from wild-harvested and dried red seaweed Phycocalidia, locally known as gamet. Gamet is an economically important species in the Philippines, with a relatively narrow distribution range in the country – that is, it is only known to occur naturally in the rocky and wave-swept coastal regions of northern Philippines.

Gracilaria sullivanii, a Philippine endemic, belongs to a group of seaweeds that are known to produce agar and agarose, both of which are commercially important derivatives with wide applications and use in various industries.

The fan-shaped Padina australis is among the few brown seaweeds known to have calcification (accumulation of calcium salts) on its thallus.

Brown seaweeds from the genus Lobophora are ecologically important species in tropical reef ecosystems. Recent studies on Lobophora suggested that the diversity of the genus is grossly underestimated, including those in the Philippines.

Sargassum currimaoense an endemic brown seaweed species that was discovered and described by Dr. Gavino C. Trono, Jr. in 1994.

Locally known as ‘balbalolang’, Hydroclathrus tenuis is a food species that widely distributed in the Indo-Pacific Ocean. The species is known to occur seasonally, sometimes in forming blooms, on shallow intertidal reefs or seagrass beds.
