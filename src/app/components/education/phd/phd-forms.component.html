PhD PROGRAM

The Ph.D. Program in Marine Science (Marine Biology, Marine Physical Sciences, and Marine Biotechnology) aims to provide advanced education on the sciences of the sea with emphasis on the dynamics of marine life. The program is designed to enable students (1) to acquire a balanced and comprehensive knowledge of the discipline, (2) to gain advanced knowledge of one area in marine biology, and (3) to develop competence in undertaking independent research in that area.

The program

Each student admitted into a Ph.D. Program shall be assigned a Program Committee composed of three (3) members to be designated by the Graduate Committee. The Ph.D. program committee shall advise, monitor, and evaluate the student until assignment of Dissertation Committee. The names of the Program Committee members shall be submitted to the Graduate Office within the first semester of the student’s initial year in the program.

Within the first semester of the student’s initial year in the PhD Program, a Program of Study shall be designed by the Program Committee, in consultation with the student and on the basis of the latter’s academic preparation and desired Graduate Committee. A copy of the approved Program of Study shall be kept in the files of the Assistant to the Deputy Director for Instruction (DDI) and the Graduate Office, College of Science.

The approved Program of Study shall be a prerequisite for further enrollment. Any revision in this Program shall have to be authorized by the Program Committee and the same shall be communicated at once to the Graduate Office through proper channels.

The normal load of formal graduate courses shall be 9 to 12 units per semester.

 HOW TO APPLY

ADMISSION

• A high degree of intellectual capacity and aptitude for research and advanced studies

• Either a Master of Science degree or its equivalent in relevant fields obtained from a recognized institution of learning

•A weighted average of at least 2.25 or its equivalent in a B.S. degree in natural sciences and mathematics (As an exception, this requirement can be waived. Reconsideration is based on research experience, publications, highly favorable recommendations from respected people in the field and/or former professors.

• An undergraduate Cumulative Weighted Average Grade (CWAG) not lower than 1.75 in the natural sciences and mathematics (In case of a straight Ph.D. program)

•A year of physics, mathematics-through calculus, chemistry-through organic chemistry, and general biology.

• Requirements for specializations:
Marine Biotechnology - Elementary biochemistry
Marine Physical Sciences - Geology 11
(Students may audit simultaneously with Physical Oceanography and Chemical Oceanography)

If the potential student is lacking only 1 course (e.g. either Organic Chemistry or Biology 11) but has passed the other entrance requirements, the student may be accepted into the program and will be required to take the course he lacks during the first semester of his enrollment as a graduate student. The students are to be reminded though that the MRR clock starts ticking the moment they enter the program.

REQUIREMENTS

Formal

• A letter of application

• Curriculum vitae

• Two (2) duly accomplished application forms

• Original and duplicate copy of undergraduate and graduate official transcript of records

•Recommendation forms from three former professors and/or supervisors with graduate degrees

• Payment of application fees

• Certification of English Proficiency - Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) average score of 500 for foreign applicants whose native language and medium of instruction is not English

•Health clearance and other special admission requirements that may be imposed by a Graduate Committee

The student shall submit the requirements above not later than six weeks before the registration period.

General

•Completion of an approved Program of Study

• Maintenance of a Cumulative Weighted Average Grade (CWAG) of not lower than 1.75 for the duration of the study

• Passing of the Qualifying Examination which is to be taken within one year after completion of the core courses in the Program of Study

•Passing of the Candidacy Examination after completion of all the course work in the student’s Program of Study

• Completion of graduate seminars (Every PhD student shall complete one seminar course every other year after 12 units of courses have been credited to his program of study, the College Research Colloquim and the yearly presentation or report of research progress requirement

• Submission of published paper based on the dissertation research

•Completion and successful public defense of a doctoral dissertation

• Submission of at least six (6) dissertation copies and CD-ROM copy of the approved dissertation

The qualifying and candidacy examinations and dissertation defense can only be scheduled from the first day of the semester until the last day of regular classes. As much as possible, it should be held on the day when most of the faculty and students are available.
