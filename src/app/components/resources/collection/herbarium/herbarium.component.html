THE G.T. VELASQUEZ PHYCOLOGICAL HERBARIUM

Established in the early 1970s, the G.T. Velasquez Phycological Herbarium is witness to the diversity of Philippine seaweeds and seagrass. It was built through the efforts of Dr. Gavino Trono, Jr. as a tribute to the late National Scientist Dr. Gregorio T. Velasquez. With over 55,000 voucher specimens, the Herbarium is the largest collection of marine macrobenthic algae and seagrass both in the Philippines and in the ASEAN region.

ABOUT DR. VELASQUEZ

Dr. Gregorio T. Velasquez was a Professor Emeritus of Botany of the University of the Philippines. He led the scientific progress of Philippine phycology with his dedication to collecting and archiving freshwater and marine algae. His 1986 publication, Blue green algae of the Philippines, contains descriptions and geographical and ecological distributions of 162 species and varieties of cyanophytes. His body of work includes 42 basic and 77 scientific papers on the subject.

The multi-awarded academician served as Chairman for Biological Sciences of the National Research Council of the Philippines and Department Chairman of the Department of Botany in the University of the Philippines. In 1982, he was recognized as a National Scientist.
